Welcome to Glenda's Homestyle Vegetarian Kitchen

Glenda is a popular not-for-profit fully-licensed vegetarian homestyle restaurant
with a 5-star rating and one of growing number of open house community kitchens located across
the North London area - dedicated in providing five-star quality home-cooked meals for the most vulnerable members in our local community.
At Glenda we are all about World foods and as such we are an eclectic group of community-minded folk with a love of cooking vegetarian-vegan cuisine.
So whether you are a vegetarian/ vegan, an omnnivore or an outright carnivore Glenda wants to infuse your taste buds with outstanding vegetarian-vegan meals.

Due to popular demand Glendas Homestyle Vegetarian Kitchen is now providing an online odering service.

About Glenda's Vegetarian Homestyle Kitchen
  • Glenda has teamed up with local growers from within our community, as well as farmsteads with perfectly good produce earmarked for destruction!
  • Glenda with a local college provides vocational training qualifications in food hygiene and safety preparation (vegetarian route) open to all volunteers as well as providing work-experience, and thus have a wealth of enthusiastic folks ready and willing to peel some carrots.
  • Glenda prepares and delivers approximately 450 free three-course meals weekly to a number of community organisations serving the most vulnerable members in and around Palmers Green.
  • Glenda caters provides seated lunches from 1200-1430 for between 30 to 40 elderly residents, at a (dependant) nominal charge of £3 each per three-course meal.
  • Glenda runs a free after-school meals programme from 1530-1700 each weekday providing meals for between 90 to 120 children between the school ages of 4 to 16 years old, at a (dependant) nominal charge of £1 each per three-course meal. This attracts over 123 families catering for between to 92 children - this figure has risen significantly.
  • Glenda operates from Lyn House - a palacious 18th century listed building. Lyn House has a history of community activity since Lady Icilda Lyn passed away in 1988 at the age of 94.
  • Lyn House has four operational kitchens, three inter-connecting dining halls each seating 40.
  • Glenda operates a successful not-for-profit fully-licensed vegetarian homestyle restaurant with a five-star rating, seating up to 40. Phone to book a reservation. See opening times.

    Donations and fundraising

  • To cover overheads Glenda asks for a (depedant) nominal fee of £1 for per child and £3 for per adult (lunch-time and after-school).
  • We tend to shy away from monetary donations - to donate we recommend you book a table at Glenda's Vegetarian Homestyle Kitchen and enjoy the setting, the cuisine and the professionalism expected of a five-star restaurant.
  • Food poverty and disparity has become an insidious part of our society and we raise awareness and funding by throwing open the manor door and inviting those who want to make a difference to (at a cost) to have a seat at the table and share a meal with us.

  • Contact Glenda to find out how else we fundraise within our community - for the betterment of our community.

    • The meal price you pay contributes towards providing free meals for the most vulnerable members across our communities.
